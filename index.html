Point of View

"The next decade of AI will be won at the seam where silicon, cloud, and the model meet. Generalist software engineers will write the applications — but the layer that decides whether the application is possible at all is infrastructure, and infrastructure is where the durable advantage lives."

Inference-time compute is the new training

The shift from training-time scaling to inference-time scaling (reasoning models, test-time compute, agentic loops) puts new pressure on GPU-cluster economics. The teams who control inference architecture control the unit economics.

Hardware-aware software wins

Frameworks that ignore the silicon underneath — memory hierarchy, kernel scheduling, NCCL behavior — leave 30-60% of GPU value on the table. Engineers who can write across the boundary are the rate-limit on AI progress.

Enterprise distribution is undervalued

The companies that win the agentic-AI era are not always the model labs — they are the firms that can put models in front of enterprise buyers with SOC2, EU AI Act compliance, and a five-9s SLO. Microsoft owns that distribution.

Agentic AI > chatbot AI

The chatbot wave was a UX trial run. The real product is autonomous agents that take actions across enterprise systems — booking, writing, coding, deploying. That product needs a stack that doesn't exist yet, and building it is the work.

Labs I Study Closely
